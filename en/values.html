Immutable foundations

Values and pillars — the AIAD constitution

The philosophy of AIAD fits in one sentence: AIAD does not judge on effort, velocity or process compliance — it judges on the value realised for stakeholders. Everything else follows from it: four pillars, seven immutable values, three founding principles.

Judging on realised value

Without an explicit definition of "succeeding", teams optimise the wrong metrics. A technically perfect feature that nobody uses is a failure. A cycle completed at 100% with no measurable impact is a failure. A partial delivery that solves the real problem is a success. AIAD states this unambiguously so that the team never confuses activity with value.

The four pillars

1. Radical empiricism. Everything is a hypothesis until proven otherwise. Real data and usage take precedence over intuitions; pivoting quickly beats persevering in error; maximising learning speed matters more than production speed. Anti-pattern: "We know what our users want, we've been working with them for five years." Yesterday's certainties are tomorrow's failures.

2. Systemic orchestration. A team's value no longer lies in its ability to code, but in its ability to orchestrate AI agents. Humans define the why and the what; agents take charge of the how. Training one expert improves one person by 20%; properly configuring the AGENT-GUIDE improves the whole team by 50%. Anti-pattern: "Our best dev doesn't need agents." Their time would be better invested configuring the ecosystem for the other ten.

3. Fluidity through emergence. Artificial cadences — fixed-length cycles — create friction with the natural velocity of AI-assisted development. AIAD replaces them with a continuous flow adjusted to actual complexity: a bug is fixed in two hours, a feature takes three days, an exploration a week. Anti-pattern: "We keep our cycles but do AIAD inside them" — the worst of both worlds: rigidity plus confusion.

4. Intentional Excellence. Shipping code is not the goal; realising an intent worthy of the value invested is. The agent can contribute to technical excellence — it cannot carry the intent. The central question: "do the outcome criteria themselves reflect an intent worthy of the value invested?" The monthly Intent Workshop — a purely human space, no AI — is the mechanism that verifies this alignment. Anti-pattern: "We ship first, we'll improve later." "Later" never comes.

The pillars form a system

You don't take "two pillars out of four". Without empiricism, you build the wrong things; without orchestration, you build slowly and badly; without fluidity, you create friction and waste; without Intentional Excellence, you ship code — not a realised intent.

The seven immutable values

1. Primacy of Human Intent. The intent of what is built remains the exclusive domain of the human. The agent executes; it does not decide what deserves to be built.

2. Honesty about Contradictions. When constraints clash, AIAD tells the truth about the trade-offs rather than promising the impossible.

3. Intentional Sobriety. We build what is necessary, not what is impressive. Every feature must justify itself with a clear intent.

4. Radical Openness. AIAD is open source: methodological knowledge is shared, never locked away. Practices, tools and field feedback are published on aiad.ovh — transparency is a condition of collective improvement.

5. Uncompromising Empiricism. Hypotheses are validated through observation, not consensus. An AIAD team does not decide on the strength of an intuition when a measurement is possible.

6. Shared Responsibility. Success and failure belong to the team, not to an individual. Responsibilities are clear — blame never is. Field illustration (2026): a Cloudflare/Stripe agent autonomously bought the domain superseal.cc instead of superseal.club — in the absence of a human checkpoint on irreversible financial effects, responsibility remains blurry. AIAD formalises this checkpoint through the Execution Gate.

7. Human Authorship. Authorship of intent cannot be delegated. Every Intent Statement is written in the first person by an identifiable human; if an agent wrote it, the session is invalidated. External legitimation (May 2026): this value corresponds precisely to Martin Fowler's "serious agentic programming" — as opposed to vibe coding — and prevents cognitive debt (Fowler/Joshi, 2026).

A system, not a menu

These seven values form a system — removing one makes the whole collapse. They are not aspirations: they are constitutive of the framework.

The three founding principles of orchestration

The 3 founding principles
PrincipleStatement
The specification as a living invariantThe SPEC remains the source of truth before, during and after implementation. It is "done" when it exactly reflects the state of the code in production. AIAD is spec-anchored: neither throwaway spec-first nor spec-as-source.
Drift as a Definition of Done failureSpec drift is not an AI problem — it is a human process failure. Any iteration that changes a behaviour updates the SPEC in the same session. That is the Drift Lock.
The Context Budget as a disciplineThe PE owns the context budget of every session. A hundred-page AGENT-GUIDE is less effective, not more: injecting everything into every session is the main anti-pattern.

Positioning in the SDD ecosystem

AIAD is a meta-framework: the tools — Kiro (Amazon), GitHub Spec-Kit, SPDD and its REASONS Canvas, OpenAI Symphony, Claude Code Routines — handle the "how to work with agents"; AIAD handles the "how to work as a human-AI team": who carries which responsibility, how to validate that the intent remains intact, how to measure real value, how to maintain regulatory governance. It can integrate these tools as implementations of its spec layer.

Relationships with ecosystem tools
ToolRelationship with AIAD
SPDDThe REASONS Canvas is an optional approach for /sdd spec. SPDD = a spec-production method; AIAD = governance of the full cycle.
OpenAI SymphonyConvergence on the persistent intent artifact — but Symphony is issue-tracker-first, with no quality criteria. AIAD adds the SQS, the Drift Lock and governance.
Routines (Claude Code)Compatible execution triggers: a Routine executes an already-validated SPEC (Gate ≥ 4/5), it does not stand in for one — no Intent, no SQS, no Human Authorship on its side.
Amazon KiroKiro starts from execution and optionally offers control; AIAD starts from human intent and builds execution on that foundation. Kiro can serve as the execution layer of a validated AIAD SPEC.

AIAD is also model-agnostic by design: Intent Statements, SPECs and the Execution Gate survive provider changes — governance lives in the artifacts, not in the vendor.

Industry validations 2026

The recent convergences are not follow-ups — they are independent validations of an earlier approach. Anthropic formalised Context Engineering as a discipline in its own right in March 2026 — AIAD has implemented it since v1.3. Thoughtworks Radar 2026 identifies Context Engineering, Harness Engineering and Spec-Driven Development as convergent concepts. R2Code (arXiv, April 2026) empirically validates spec-first: −41.7% of tokens and +7.4% fidelity. AWS Strands (2026) observes −96% of tokens on intent-based workflows. Gartner MQ 2026 projects 30-50% agentic productivity gains by 2028 — creating the governance vacuum that AIAD addresses structurally.
