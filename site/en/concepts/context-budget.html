Principle #3 · /sdd context

Context Engineering Budget — the discipline of context

The Product Engineer owns the context budget of every agent session. An agent does not degrade its quality at the theoretical limit of its window — it degrades it well before. The Context Engineering Budget is the discipline that turns this physical reality into operational rules.

Context rot — degradation before the limit

Context rot refers to the degradation of LLM quality well before the theoretical limit of the context window. The symptoms are recognisable: less accurate answers, forgotten spec constraints, repetitive behaviour. A hundred-page AGENT-GUIDE is not more effective — it is less effective: the agent receives everything, understands little. That is why injecting all artifacts at once is the framework's main anti-pattern.

In March 2026, Anthropic formalised Context Engineering as a discipline in its own right, distinct from prompt engineering — "the art and science of curating what enters the limited context window". The AIAD Context Budget is an operational implementation of it, present in the framework since v1.3.

The operational threshold: 60-70% of the window

Rule v1.6

Use 60-70% of the available context as the effective maximum. Example: 200k tokens available → max budget ≈ 130k. Beyond that, context-rot symptoms appear before the theoretical limit.

Model reference v1.6
ModelEffective windowOperational threshold
Opus 4.81M effective tokens60-70% — shifted upwards
Sonnet 4.6200k tokens60-70%
Haiku 4.5200k tokens60-70%

The key point: the available window does not change the 60-70% threshold — it shifts it upwards. A one-million-token window is no excuse to drop the discipline; it only widens the margin.

The session composition rules

Context Engineering Budget rules
RuleDetail
Permanent contextCondensed AGENT-GUIDE + ARCHITECTURE summary — max 500 tokens each
Per-task activationONE active SPEC at a time (unless an explicit combined activation scope is declared)
Strategic contextThe full PRD is injected only during the framing phase, never during development
Placement ruleIntent Statement and active SPEC at the top of the context, against the "lost in the middle effect"

The "lost in the middle effect" is documented: LLMs pay more attention to the beginning and end of their context than to the middle. Placing the intent and the SPEC at the top guarantees that what must not be betrayed stays in the zone of best adherence.

The economic argument

The discipline of context is not just a quality matter — it is a measurable cost-optimisation decision. Two converging data points validate intent-based design: R2Code (arXiv, April 2026) measures −41.7% of tokens consumed on spec-anchored code, with +7.4% fidelity; AWS Strands (2026) observes −96% of tokens on intent-based workflows. On code-generation-intensive projects, the saving is directly measurable — and model-agnostic: it applies regardless of the platform. The /sdd context command provides an estimate of the cost avoided per session.

The context-budget skill and the 5 health metrics

The post-session audit is tooled by the context-budget skill, invoked by /sdd context, /sdd exec (budget check) and /sdd resume. It computes five metrics and produces a health score out of 5:

The 5 health metrics M1 to M5
MetricDefinitionTarget
M1 — Useful-utilisation rate% of context actually referenced by the agent / total injected context≥ 70%
M2 — Restart frequencyNumber of /compact or resumes0 ideal, 1 acceptable
M3 — Estimate/actual ratioActual context / estimated0.8 – 1.3
M4 — Coherence scoreAGENT-GUIDE constraints respected / total≥ 90%
M5 — Session durationActive minutes< 35 min

Each off-target metric points to a precise remediation: M1 < 70% → too much context injected; M4 dropping → context rot, condense the AGENT-GUIDE; M5 > 35 min → objective too broad, go through /sdd split. If a metric is not measurable, the score comes out as ?/5 — a JNSP, not a zero.

A single session < 35 minutes, then /compact

The practical rule that sums it all up: one session = one objective, lasting less than 35 minutes, context = AGENT-GUIDE + condensed ARCHITECTURE + active SPEC. Beyond 35 minutes, run /compact and restart on a clean session — a session that drags on is the signal of a SPEC that is too broad or a poorly prepared context, not of a slow agent. The /sdd resume command then rebuilds a minimal, precise context from the summary of the interrupted session, without re-injecting everything.
