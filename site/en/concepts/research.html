Cycle step 2 · /sdd research

Research Phase — validating intent viability

The Research phase (v1.18, §3.5) sits between the Intent Statement and the SPEC. It does not judge the quality of a specification — that is the SQS's role — but answers an earlier question: is this intent achievable, at what cost, with what risks, given the actual code?

Why a step between the Intent and the SPEC

Without a Research phase, the cycle jumps straight from intent to specification: you describe how to build something without ever checking that the terrain allows it. That is the "specs-to-code" anti-pattern: SPECs written in a vacuum, disconnected from existing constraints — established patterns, accumulated debt, real dependencies. The agent discovers those constraints mid-execution, and the session drifts.

Research reverses the order of operations: before a single requirement is written, it maps the code involved, assesses feasibility against the current architecture, and lists the risks and unknowns. The result is archived in .aiad/research/RESEARCH-NNN-<slug>.md, always attached to a parent Intent — a Research is anchored to a human WHY, never to a free-floating idea.

What Research is not

It does not score the SPEC (that is the Execution Gate's role), does not replace the Intent Statement (the WHY remains human), and does not decide in the human's place: Research informs, the human decides.

The Discovery — mandatory anchoring in the code

The heart of Research is the Discovery: a map of the impacted code, delegated to a read-only Explore agent to preserve the main session's context budget. Every identified area must be cited as path:line (or evidence: …), together with its existing constraints and the test surface already in place.

## Discovery (code anchoring — Explore agent, read-only)

- Impacted files / areas: src/auth/login.ts:42, evidence: …
- Existing constraints: patterns, debt, dependencies in place
- Existing test surface: test/auth/*.test.ts

The rule is hard by design: without at least one real anchor in the code, the machine verdict stays JNSP (Je Ne Sais Pas — "I don't know"). A Research without a Discovery is an opinion, not an analysis. Any unknown that requires a human decision is recorded as TODO-JNSP: <question> — it never hides behind a confident GO.

A graduated verdict, decided by the human

Research concludes with a verdict line that only an identifiable human can set (human-authorship-check skill). The agent gathers the facts, presents the options, may even offer a recommendation — but never manufactures the decision. The verdict is then hardened by a deterministic scorer: npx aiad-sdd research RESEARCH-NNN.

Semantics of the four Research verdicts
Human decisionMachine verdictExitRest of the cycle
GOPASS0/sdd spec allowed — no blocking unknowns
CONDITIONAL GOCONDITIONAL0/sdd spec allowed, explicit conditions to clear
DEFERFAIL1Postponed (dependency, timing, missing info) — new Research required
NO-GOFAIL1Abandoned (cost or risk too high) — new Research required
Empty Discovery · open TODO-JNSP · missing verdictJNSP2Human decision required before continuing

A CONDITIONAL GO with no conditions listed is rejected by the scorer (JNSP verdict): an implicit condition is an unknown in disguise. Every verdict comes with a confidence level expressed as a percentage — a GO at 60% confidence says something different from a GO at 95%.

Human Authorship — non-negotiable

If the Verdict: line has no identifiable human author, the agent does not set it and exits with JNSP. Manufacturing a GO/NO-GO in the human's place is exactly the kind of invention the framework forbids.

The discovery-gate.js hook — a verified prerequisite

Research is not a polite recommendation: it is a mechanically verified prerequisite. The .aiad/hooks/discovery-gate.js hook is consulted by /sdd spec and /sdd exec: without a Research linked to the Intent, with an anchored Discovery and a GO or CONDITIONAL GO verdict, SPEC writing and agent launch are refused.

npx aiad-sdd research <RESEARCH-id>   # exit 0 → OK · 1 → DEFER/NO-GO · 2 → JNSP

JNSP — Missing Discovery for INTENT-NNN
What is missing: a linked Research with an anchored Discovery (path:line)
                 and a GO/CONDITIONAL GO verdict.
Question for the human: run `/sdd research INTENT-NNN` first.

A DEFER or a NO-GO closes the door to /sdd spec durably: it takes a new Research to reopen it. This is consistent with the framework's empiricism — if the context has changed, you demonstrate it again on evidence; you do not recycle a stale verdict.

Proportionality — a safeguard, not bureaucracy

Not every intent deserves a full Research. For a trivial, risk-free intent, the Product Engineer may explicitly bypass the prerequisite — provided the decision is traced in the SPEC. A silent bypass, however, is forbidden: the trace is what distinguishes a deliberate choice from an oversight.

The aiad-sdd proportionality <INTENT-id> command recommends the short path (light Research) or the heavy path (full Research) depending on the risk level. In --guided mode, the grill-me skill probes the GO/NO-GO one question at a time, with a recommendation the human validates or corrects.

In practice

A label fix does not justify an hour-long Research; an overhaul of the authentication flow does. The right reflex: --fast for clear, low-risk intents; guided mode and an in-depth Discovery as soon as the affected code is critical.
